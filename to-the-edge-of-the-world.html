PROJECT / TO THE EDGE OF THE WORLD (CAVE)

To the Edge of the World

A CAVE room experience on a motion platform about a perilous sea journey.

Producer · Programmer · Technical Artist CAVE + Vive Tracker 2-week project · 2021/10/9–11/2
Original Wix Page ↗ Download (GitHub) ↗

Illustration for the CAVE experience: a giant sea creature, a lighthouse and a ship at the edge of the world.

Overview

To the Edge of the World is a short narrative experience set in CMU’s CAVE room. Guests stand on a motion platform surrounded by projections that simulate travelling at sea. They join a crew of three sailors on a quest to find the “edge of the world”, encountering monsters, storms and moments of doubt along the way.

Theme & Setup

The assigned theme was to guide the guest through an interactive story using experimental platforms. We combined:

  • CAVE: a room-sized space with three projection walls and a motion platform on the floor.
  • Vive Trackers: used to track the boat’s movement and the guest’s position for camera control.
  • Improv actors: performers in the room who interact with the guest and reinforce story beats.

Role & Contributions

  • As producer, coordinated a two-week schedule and kept art, design and tech aligned.
  • As programmer, implemented CAVE integration, tracker-based camera logic and interaction events.
  • As technical artist, tuned lighting, water shaders and visual effects to support the narrative beats.

Experience Flow

The story begins with excitement as the sailors chase a mythic destination. As they repeatedly pass the same lighthouse, tension rises and one crew member begins to doubt the captain. The guest is placed right in the middle of this conflict, surrounded by waves, sounds and performers.

Reflection

Building for a CAVE space pushed me to think beyond the headset and treat the entire room as a medium. I learned how important it is to coordinate timing between projections, physical motion, acting and sound design so the audience feels fully immersed.
