PROJECT / SHAPETOPIA

Shapetopia

A WebGL policymaking card game about sustainable development.

Lead Programmer Unity · WebGL Feb–May 2022
Original Wix Page ↗ Play the Game ↗ Project Website ↗ CMU News Article ↗

Shapetopia is a colourful city-building card game about sustainable policy choices.

Overview

Shapetopia is a browser-based card game that invites players to explore how policies interact and shape a city. Each policy card affects multiple indicators such as environment, economy and citizens’ wellbeing. By combining cards, players discover trade-offs and synergies, and are encouraged to reflect on how these patterns mirror real-world decisions.

Role & Team

I worked as the lead programmer on a team of six, collaborating with designers and artists to turn our paper prototypes into a polished WebGL experience.

  • Designed and implemented the core game systems in Unity and C#.
  • Coordinated the programming task breakdown and sprint planning.
  • Helped define how card mechanics communicate complex policy ideas in a playful way.

Platform & Tools

  • Platform: WebGL (playable in modern browsers).
  • Engine: Unity3D, C# scripting.
  • Collaboration: Git, Perforce and Google Suite for version control and documentation.

Design Goals

Our design focused on three main goals:

  • Encourage players to see policies as interconnected, rather than isolated decisions.
  • Support reflection: help players connect in-game outcomes to real-world sustainability challenges.
  • Spark conversation between players, classmates and teachers about how to make cities more liveable and equitable.

Media & Playtesting

Playtesting with students and featuring the project on CMU’s Earth Day news story helped us iterate and reach a wider audience.

Impact & Takeaways

Shapetopia showed me how system design and UI details can make complex topics approachable. Building a game that teachers can use in class also strengthened my skills in documentation, deployment and long-term maintainability for non-technical stakeholders.
